Four Angels is dedicated to making a difference in the lives of those who need it most. We are committed to providing high-quality care, education, and resources to help individuals achieve their goals.

Our Team

Jordan Anderson

President

Hi! My name is Jordan and I am the President of our board at Four Angels. I live in Laramie with my husband, our four daughters, and our dogs. I started Four Angels after my husband and I experienced several losses. I wanted a way to turn our grief into something meaningful to others who have experienced pregnancy loss.

Nikki McConnell

Vice President

My name is Nikki McConnell. My husband, Sean and I have two wonderful children, Addilyn and Clive. We lived in Laramie for 11 years before moving to Wisconsin to help with a church revitalization. As a former Labor and Delivery nurse I had the honor of walking with families through both their best and worst days.

Sarah Hallet

Secretary

Hi! My name is Sarah. I am a mom of three beautiful girls (two bio one bonus), and a daycare owner. A few years ago I suffered a missed miscarriage and it was one of the hardest times in my life. Because of this experience I decided that I wanted to help Jordan out with Four Angels non-profit in order to support other women who have had a miscarriage.

Randi Teel

Member at Large

Hi all! My name is Randi and I live in Laramie with my husband on a small farm. We have horses, chickens, dogs, and cats so my time is always full! I am a member at large on the board for Four Angels and take pride in knowing that I am helping to provide support and resources to those who have experienced loss as I personally know the pain they are experiencing.

Carly

Member at Large

I live in wonderful Laramie, Wyoming, and I’m honored to be a part of such a powerful group. I’ve been blessed with two amazing children and didn’t have the hardship of my own loss. But having multiple friends who have had personal losses, I want to be a voice for those who stand with our friends, help others who witness their friends’ grief, and advocate for those who want to help but don’t quite know how.
